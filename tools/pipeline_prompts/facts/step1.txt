<task>
Propose 12 candidate Facts topics, then select the strongest {{BATCH_SIZE}} to write. A good
Facts topic is a single verifiable, counterintuitive truth with a reframe that overturns an

<method>
Before proposing:
1. Read the canonical tag taxonomy in @backend/seed.py, the examples in
   @docs/content-structure/examples/, and what has already been generated in
   @docs/content-structure/generated/facts/ (scan the latter by filename, tags, and field, not
   in full). Note which taxonomy areas already have a Facts post and which are empty, so the
   candidates spread coverage instead of clustering. Avoid any topic close to an existing or
   already-generated post; the seed upserts on filename, so a repeat would publish a duplicate.
2. Web-search to confirm each candidate is real and well-sourced. Drop anything you cannot
   ground in primary or strong secondary sources, and anything whose core claim is actually
   disputed, unless the dispute itself is the fact.

For each selected topic, also decide a distinct WAY IN (the angle or entry the post will take)
and vary it across the batch, so the posts do not all open the same way. Also read
@docs/content-structure/generated/facts/_recent_moves.md (a running tally of the opening, closing,
and story-pivot shapes earlier batches used, with counts; it may not exist for the first batch) and
steer this batch's way-ins away from them, avoiding hardest the shapes with the highest counts. See
@docs/content-structure/CROSS_POST_VARIANCE.md for what the moves are and the file's format.
This is the cross-batch variance lever; heading sameness off here is cheapest. Also vary the topic ARCHETYPE, the kind of fact each post is, not just its way-in: for example a myth or misconception corrected, a test-it-on-yourself experience, a mechanism or feedback loop explained, a surprising scale or number, a reasoning or statistical trap, a delayed or overlooked discovery. Two posts of the same archetype tend to rhyme at the story and the close even when their way-ins differ, so avoid stacking the same archetype within a batch and, reading _recent_moves and what is already generated, avoid piling one archetype batch after batch. This is a SOFT preference, not a quota: a single batch may hold kindred topics, and areas with richer or more interesting material may carry more posts. Aim for a reasonable spread of archetypes and taxonomy areas over time, not forced uniformity.
</method>
